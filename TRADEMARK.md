# AnthraCode Trademark Policy

The AnthraCode **source code** is open source under the MIT License. The
AnthraCode **name and brand** are not. This policy explains how you may and may
not use them.

## What this covers

"AnthraCode", the AnthraCode word mark, and the AnthraCode logo (the "Marks") are
trademarks of the AnthraCode project. The MIT License grants you broad rights to
the code; it does **not** grant any right to use the Marks.

## You may, without asking

- State, truthfully, that your project "uses AnthraCode", "is built with
  AnthraCode", or "is compatible with AnthraCode".
- Use the name to refer to the genuine, unmodified AnthraCode software.
- Link to the official project and npm package.

## You may not, without written permission

- Use "AnthraCode" (or a confusingly similar name) as the name of your own
  product, fork, service, company, or domain.
- Use the Marks in a way that suggests official status, sponsorship, or
  endorsement by the AnthraCode project when none exists.
- Modify the software and continue to distribute it under the "AnthraCode" name
  in a way that could confuse users about its origin. If you ship a modified
  build, give it your own name and make clear it is a derivative.
- Use the logo in altered colors, proportions, or composition.

## Forks

You are free to fork the MIT-licensed code. If you distribute a modified
version, please **rename it** so users are not misled into thinking it is the
official AnthraCode, and retain the upstream copyright notices (see `NOTICE`).

## Relationship to OpenCode

AnthraCode is itself a fork of OpenCode. "OpenCode" is the trademark of its
respective owners; this policy does not grant rights to the OpenCode name or
marks.
